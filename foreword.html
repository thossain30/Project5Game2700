Foreword

The morning sun hits your face, and you roll over. A look at the clock shows that it’s 8am. Bright and early on the wedding day. You’re in a hotel bed anxious about the wedding. Only thing is, it's not your wedding. And you’re in love with the bride.

You were there the night Caley and Joseph met, three years ago. You and Joseph were at a Fourth of July party, and Caley met you both, a little sunburned but smiling. You fell in love with her the moment you saw her. And she fell in love with Joseph. You and Caley shared a drunken kiss shortly after the engagement, something you’ve never shared with anyone. And here you are, about to see the love of your life marry a friend. An acquaintance, really. Only invited because you were there when they met.

You sit up in bed, rub the sleep out of your eyes. You check your phone. There’s a text, from 3 am last night. From Caley, of all people: “I need to talk to you”. You know, more certainly than you have ever known anything, that you have to stop this wedding.

Find Caley and tell her you love her

or
